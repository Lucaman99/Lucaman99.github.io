Why Continuous Variables?

At an intuitive level, I think that the best way to motivate the use of quantum computing over continuous variables is that we are able to exploit observables with a continuous spectrum, rahter than remaining in a "discret realm". Let's back up for one second. Within quantum mechanics, every quantity that we wish to measure is associated with some observable, which is just an operator acting on our complex Hilbert space of state vectors. For instance, in basic quantum mechanics, some of the observables we deal with quite a bit are position, momentum, and energy. Each observable has a corresponding spectrum, which can be thought of as the collection of values associated with that particular observable that we are allowed to measure.

We can categorize the spectrum of each physical observable as continuous or discrete.
