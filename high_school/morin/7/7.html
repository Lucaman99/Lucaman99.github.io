Part A
Since the impact parameter gives us the vertical distance from the x-axis to the asymptote of the parabola. Part B
Let us begin by noting that: $$\frac{d\sigma}{d\Omega} \ = \ \frac{d\sigma}{db} \frac{db}{d\Omega} \ = \ \frac{d\sigma}{db} \frac{d\phi}{d\Omega} \frac{db}{d\phi}$$ Alternatively, if you don't care about rigour, we can recognize that in infinitesimally small patch of area on the sphere of radius $D$ will be given by $D^2 (d\phi)^2$, so we will have $d\Omega \ = \ d\phi^2$. We also know that for the infinitesimal cross-sectional patch, the impact parameter at one side of the square will be $b$, while the impact parameter at the other side will be $b \ + \ db$. This implies that the length of the patch is $db$, which means that $d\sigma \ = \ db^2$. This gives us: $$\frac{d\sigma}{d\Omega} \ = \ \frac{db^2}{d\phi^2} \ = \ \Big( \frac{db}{d\phi} \Big)^2 \ = \ \frac{1}{4 \omega^2 \sin^{4} (\phi/2)}$$ For all the mathematicians in the audience, if you want to take a moment to gouge your eyes out after seeing that "proof", I wouldn't blame you...
