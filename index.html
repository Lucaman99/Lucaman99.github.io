About Me

Hello there! My name is Jack, and I'm a high school student from the Greater Toronto Area with interests in mathematics, physics, engineering and quantum computing. In my free time, I like to:

  • Entangle qubits (fake ones, I don't have enough money to buy the real thing...)
  • Build a robot army (my ranks currently have two soldiers, both of which fall apart if left running for over 5 minutes)
  • "Prove" "theorems"
  • Solve hopelessly long integrals to find expectation values
  • Set digital trees on fire with my homemade computer simulations

If you share any of these interests, please feel free to reach out! If you don't share these interests, also feel free to reach out! I'm always looking for new interesting friends/academic colleagues!

Other than doing the stuff on the above list, I have been incredibly lucky to have a lot of cool experiences that have allowed me to develop my knowledge further in the past few years!

This includes taking part in a pure mathematics project at the University of Toronto, being a co-contributor to a quantum computing textbook (it's actually not that impressive, there are a lot of co-authors :) ), being a Qiskit advocate and having the extremely aweosme opportunity to take part in the ISSYP Program at the Perimeter Institute for Theoretical Physics.

I'm currently working on a bunch of projects in quantum computing, mathematics, and physics. The purpose of this website is to hopefully contain a (mostly) up-to-date log of all the stuff I'm working on!
