About Me







Projects

I am a high school student from the Greater Toronto Area with a great interest in mathematics, physics, and quantum computing. I love working on projects that leverage the beauty and abstraction within pure mathematics, and physics, as I believe that these diciplines are where true artistry and creative thought lie. I also like to use these fields to solve large, important problems in the world. I am always looking for new projects that involve my interests, so please feel free to reach out!



Quora Writing

Over the course of the past few years, I have answered over 150 answers about mathematics and physics on the online question/answers website, Quora. I have also written numerous blog posts about topics within math, physics, and quantum computing. A link to my profile can be found here.


Brilliant.org Writing

I have posted numerous questions and solutions to problems in the mathematics, physics, and computer science website, Brilliant.org. Most recently, I created a problem set called the 12 Days of Mathmas, where every other day in December up until Christmas Even, I posted a new problem. I covered a wide variety of topics, such as classical mechanics, vector calculus, optimization, and even group theory. A link to my profile can be found here.


Quantum Fourier Transform

I was able to use Google's quantum computing framework to simulate the Quantum Fourier Transform, and apply it to two other algorithms: quantum phase estimation and quantum order finding. A link to the post explaining my project can be found here.


Deutsch's Algorithm Simulation

I created a simulation of Deutsch's quantum algorithm using the Cirq framework, which is Google's quantum programming library. This was the first project I ever did with quantum computing, so it was an extraordinary learning experience. A link to the article explaining my project can be found here.


Forest Fire Simulation

For the past several months, along with a friend, I have been working on a mathematical and physical model, a cellular automata simulation, and a data-gathering robot that can help to predict how forest fires will spread within a forest. The robot can also detect fires, and send back coordinates of the flame in real time. A link to an article published on the Tech Under 20 website about this project can be found here.
